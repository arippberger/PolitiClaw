# Copy guide

Guidance for anyone writing user-facing PolitiClaw copy — docs, tool descriptions, skill frontmatter, alert strings, UI.

## Canonical product promise

> PolitiClaw is a **local-first civic copilot that holds your representatives accountable to the values you declare.** It learns what you care about, watches federal legislation and elections for you, and flags when your reps' votes and actions align — or don't — with your stated stances. It drafts letters you send yourself; it never speaks on your behalf and never tells you how to vote.

This is the reference text. Shorter surfaces (tagline, tool description, skill frontmatter) should be consistent with it.

## Canonical tagline

> **Holds your representatives accountable to the values you declare.**

Use this verbatim for the plugin manifest description lead and the homepage hero. Do not substitute "helps you keep track of," "monitors politics," or "political copilot" — those revert to the pre-accountability framing.

## Style rules

- **Lead with accountability, not lookup.** Name *the user's values* and *their reps' behavior* in the same sentence when possible. Avoid "a tool for looking up bills," "stay informed about Congress," "everything you need to know about politics."
- **Accountability is always to the user's own declared stances.** Never to an external political agenda. This is how accountability copy coexists with the non-prescriptive rules in monitoring, ballot, and summary skills.
- **Product copy may be pointed; skill *output* stays factual.** "Holds accountable," "watches the gap," "measures where they represent you" are fine in positioning copy. Skill output still follows the no-cheerleading, no-prescriptive-verbs rules (no "you should," no "vote YES/NO," no "exciting news!").
- **Preserve explicit scope honesty.** Federal coverage. US House roll-call votes today; Senate ingest pending; no state/local legislation; local-first; draft-only outreach; no send path. Never imply Senate vote coverage, state legislation coverage, or a send-on-your-behalf path that does not exist.
- **Keep the draft-only / user-sends boundary visible** wherever outreach appears.

## Reconciling accountability with neutrality

Users will read "holds accountable" as a strong claim. The reconciliation is one sentence: **accountability here is against the stances the user themselves declared, not against an external political agenda.** That is why the non-prescriptive, no-cheerleading rules in the skills are compatible with the accountability framing in product copy — the skills enforce "no position we invented for you," not "no position at all."
